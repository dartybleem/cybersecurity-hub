What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity protects systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. It includes network, cloud, app security, cryptography, penetration testing, digital forensics, AI monitoring, and cyber-physical protection.

Essential for finance, healthcare, government, energy, transport, and education. Every digital interaction depends on secure systems.

  • Confidentiality: Data for authorized users only.
  • Integrity: Data accuracy and reliability.
  • Availability: Systems remain operational.
  • Non-repudiation: Actions cannot be denied.
  • Accountability: System actions are traceable.

How Cybersecurity Works

  • Network Security: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs, segmentation, protocols.
  • Endpoint Security: Antivirus, EDR, patching.
  • Application Security: Code audits, encryption, penetration testing.
  • Data Security: Backups, cloud monitoring, secure storage.
  • IAM: MFA, SSO, role-based access.
  • Monitoring & Response: SIEM, SOC, anomaly detection, recovery.
  • Training: Awareness, phishing simulations, policy enforcement.

Cyber Threats Explained

  • Malware: Viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware causing data theft or disruption.
  • Phishing: Social engineering via email/SMS/fake sites.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts files, demands payment.
  • DDoS: Overloads servers, disrupts services.
  • Insider Threats: Malicious or careless insiders.
  • APT: Targeted, long-term attacks for espionage/IP theft.
  • IoT & Zero-Day: Exploits unpatched devices/software.
