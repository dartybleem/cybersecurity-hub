What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity protects systems, networks, devices, applications, and data from digital attacks. It spans network security, cloud security, app security, digital forensics, ethical hacking, AI threat analysis, blockchain integrity, intrusion detection, access control, and cyber-physical protection.

Critical for healthcare, finance, transportation, governance, military, education, and communication. Protects privacy, national security, and business continuity.

  • Confidentiality — authorized access only.
  • Integrity — accurate, unaltered information.
  • Availability — systems operational when needed.
  • Non-repudiation — actions cannot be denied.
  • Accountability — all actions traceable.

How Cybersecurity Works

Cybersecurity uses layers of hardware, software, processes, and human vigilance:

  • Network Security: Firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs, segmentation.
  • Endpoint Security: Antivirus, EDR, device management, patches.
  • Application Security: Code audits, penetration testing, encryption, secure APIs.
  • Data Security: Backups, encryption, cloud monitoring, tokenization.
  • Identity & Access Management: Authentication, SSO, MFA, role-based access.
  • Monitoring & Response: SIEM, SOC teams, AI anomaly detection, recovery plans.
  • Training: Awareness, phishing simulations, policy enforcement.

Cyber Threats Explained

Categories of cyber threats. Users research real-life examples:

  • Malware: Viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware.
  • Phishing: Emails, SMS, fake websites.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts files, demands payment.
  • DDoS: Floods servers to disrupt service.
  • Insider Threats: Malicious or careless insiders.
  • APT: Long-term targeted attacks for espionage.
  • IoT/Zero-Day: Exploits unknown device/software flaws.
